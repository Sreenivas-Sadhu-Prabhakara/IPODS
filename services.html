01 / MILL · TURN · 3·4·5-AXIS

CNC Machining

The core competency the rest of the shop is built around. Three-, four- and five-axis milling and turning — including mill-turn — in aluminum, titanium, stainless and engineering plastics, from a single fixture plate to a production run.

Five-axis lets a part be cut in fewer set-ups, which is how features stay referenced to the same datums and how size holds across a lot. Every program is planned around the working tolerance: ±0.0005 in (0.013 mm).

02 / EDM-W · SINK

Wire & Sinker EDM

Electrical discharge machining removes metal with sparks, not a cutting edge — so there's no cutting force to deflect a thin wall and no tool pressure on a hardened part. It's how the shop reaches sharp internal corners, tight slots and profiles that a rotating tool simply can't.

EDM-W

Wire EDM

A charged wire cuts a continuous profile clean through the stock — dies, punches, splines and intricate through-features, in hardened tool steel included.

SINK

Sinker / Die-Sinking

A shaped electrode burns a blind cavity to form — deep ribs, keyways, square-bottom pockets and mold detail that can't be reached by a drill or an end mill.

Request EDM Best for hardened steel, sharp internal corners and burr-sensitive features.

04 / QA-CMM

Quality Assurance

Under an ISO 9001:2015 system, conformance is verified — not assumed. Coordinate-measuring-machine inspection confirms dimensions against the print, and material traceability follows the metal from certificate to shipped part.

THE CHAIN OF CUSTODY — GENERALLY TRUE OF AN ISO SHOP:

  • OP.10 Incoming — material received against its MTR (mill test report) and logged.
  • OP.20 In-process — checks at the machine hold size across the run.
  • OP.30 Final CMM — dimensional verification against the drawing.
  • OP.40 CoC — the lot ships with a Certificate of Conformance and traceability.
